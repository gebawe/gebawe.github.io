Research and Technology Development of Perception models for Autonomous Vehicles

I have a big interest and passion in Cutting–Edge Technology i.e. Computer Vision, Deep Learning, and its application in Autonomous Vehicles. It had grown since I was a little boy when I had the opportunity to play with an old radio, dynamo, and other electronic devices. My curiosity about new technology and ego to know more also arise as I try to build some device that can make my life easier. In 2020 I received my EMJMD (IPCV) Image Processing and Computer Vision Masters degree from Universite De Bordeaux, Bordeaux, France. This has allowed me to specialize in my experts, observe, and propose a solution to solve my community and the world's problem. I thought it would not just be an exciting experience but also something that would broaden my mindset and understanding towards a great Achievement.

3D Semantic-Segmentation for Lidar-based Autonomous Vehicle Perception.


This project developed a deep learning model for 3D Semantic Segmentation

  • Spatio-Temporal Fusion of Sequential Lidar data
  • 3D Semantic Segmentation of Sequential Lidar data into ~20 classes including: (i.e., "Car", "Truck", "Bus", "Motorcyclist", "Bicyclist", "Pedestrian", "Sign", "Traffic Light", "Pole", "Construction Cone", "Bicycle", "Motorcycle", "Building", "Vegetation", "Tree Trunk", "Curb", "Road", "Lane Marker", "Walkable", "Sidewalk")

    • Using only 20% of manual labels, This work achieved State-of-the-art in 3D Semantic Segmentation using semi-supervised learning and also outperforms some of the fully supervised methods.
